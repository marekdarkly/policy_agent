## Overview

A Power of Attorney (PoA) allows a trusted person (the "attorney") to manage financial affairs on behalf of someone who may not be able to do so themselves. ToggleBank supports registered attorneys and provides safeguards to protect our customers.

## Types of Power of Attorney

| Type | Jurisdiction | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Lasting Power of Attorney — Property & Financial Affairs (LPA)** | England & Wales | Allows the attorney to manage bank accounts, pay bills, and make financial decisions. Can be used while the donor still has mental capacity (if specified) or only when they lose capacity. |
| **Enduring Power of Attorney (EPA)** | England & Wales | An older form of PoA created before 1 October 2007. Remains valid if properly registered with the Office of the Public Guardian. |
| **Continuing Power of Attorney** | Scotland | Scottish equivalent of an LPA for financial affairs. |
| **Deputyship Order** | England & Wales | Court-appointed authority when no PoA is in place. Issued by the Court of Protection. |

## Registering a Power of Attorney with ToggleBank

To register a PoA with us, the attorney will need to provide:

- The **original or certified copy** of the registered LPA/EPA/Continuing PoA or Deputyship Order
- **Photo ID** for the attorney (passport or UK driving licence)
- A completed **ToggleBank Third-Party Access form** (available online or in-branch)

### Registration Process

1. **Submit documents** — In-branch (preferred) or by post to: Power of Attorney Team, ToggleBank, PO Box 4567, Leeds, LS1 2TW.
2. **Verification** — ToggleBank reviews the documents and validates the PoA with the Office of the Public Guardian register.
3. **Confirmation** — Access is granted within **5–10 working days** of receiving valid documents.
4. **Access set up** — The attorney receives their own login credentials for Online Banking (where the LPA permits this).

Original documents are returned by **registered post within 5 working days** or can be collected in-branch.

## What an Attorney Can Do

Once registered, an attorney can typically:

- View account balances and statements
- Make payments and transfers
- Set up, amend, or cancel Direct Debits and standing orders
- Apply for or close accounts (subject to terms)
- Correspond with ToggleBank on the donor's behalf
- Withdraw cash from branches (with ID)

## What an Attorney Cannot Do

An attorney **cannot**:

- Change the donor's registered address without supporting evidence
- Open accounts for personal benefit
- Make gifts or donations from the donor's account beyond what is customary (e.g. birthday gifts) unless the LPA specifically authorises this
- Close all accounts and take funds for personal use
- Override any specific restrictions stated in the LPA document

## Third-Party Access (Without a PoA)

If you want someone to help manage your banking but do not need a formal Power of Attorney:

| Access Type | Description | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Nominated contact** | A trusted person we can discuss your account with | Enquiries only — cannot make transactions |
| **Third-party mandate** | Authorisation for someone to operate your account | Full transactional access (or limited, as you specify) |
| **Carer's card** | A secondary debit card for a carer to make purchases on your behalf | Spending up to an agreed daily limit |

Third-party mandates can be set up in-branch and require ID from both the account holder and the third party.

## Removing a Power of Attorney

A PoA registration with ToggleBank can be removed:

- By the **donor** (while they have mental capacity) — written instruction to ToggleBank
- By the **attorney** — written notice of resignation
- By the **Office of the Public Guardian** — if the PoA is revoked or the attorney is removed
- Upon **death** of the donor — ToggleBank is notified via the bereavement process

## Safeguarding Measures

ToggleBank takes the protection of vulnerable customers extremely seriously:

- All PoA transactions are **monitored** for unusual patterns.
- Large or unusual withdrawals may trigger a **safeguarding review**.
- Staff are trained to recognise signs of **financial abuse**.
- Concerns are escalated to the **Safeguarding Team** and, where appropriate, reported to the **Office of the Public Guardian** or **Adult Social Services**.
- If we suspect the attorney is acting outside their authority, we may **suspend access** while we investigate.
