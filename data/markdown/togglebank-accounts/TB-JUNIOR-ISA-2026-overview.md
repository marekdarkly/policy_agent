# ToggleBank Junior Cash ISA

**Product Code:** TB-JUNIOR-ISA-2026
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
**Account Type:** Junior Individual Savings Account (Cash)

---

## Overview

The ToggleBank Junior Cash ISA is a **tax-free savings account for children under 18**, helping parents, grandparents, and family friends build a financial foundation for a child's future. With a competitive interest rate and the security of FSCS protection, it's a simple and effective way to save for a child's education, first car, university costs, or whatever they choose when they turn 18.

## Key Features

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Interest rate | **4.00% AER (tax-free, variable)** |
| Annual allowance | **£9,000** (2025/26 tax year) |
| Eligible age | **Under 18** |
| Who can open | Parent or legal guardian |
| Who can contribute | **Anyone** — parents, grandparents, family, friends |
| Access before 18 | **Not permitted** (except in limited circumstances) |
| Access at 18 | Child gains full control |
| Interest payment | Annual (credited to the JISA) |
| FSCS protection | Yes — up to £85,000 |

## Tax-Free Savings

All interest earned in a Junior Cash ISA is **completely free of UK income tax**. Unlike a standard children's savings account, there are no tax implications regardless of who contributes or how much interest is earned.

| Scenario | Standard Savings Account | Junior Cash ISA |
|---|---|---|
| Interest earned | May be taxable (if >£100 from parental gifts) | **Always tax-free** |
| Annual limit | No limit | £9,000 per tax year |
| Access before 18 | Depends on account type | Not permitted |

## Contributions

- The **annual subscription limit** is £9,000 for the 2025/26 tax year (6 April 2025 – 5 April 2026).
- Contributions can come from **anyone** — there is no restriction on who pays in.
- Contributions can be made by:
  - Bank transfer
  - Standing order
  - Cheque
  - Cash deposit in branch
- The £9,000 limit is shared across all Junior ISAs the child holds (cash and stocks & shares combined).

## Who Can Open an Account?

| Role | Can Open? | Can Contribute? | Can Manage? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Parent / legal guardian | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Grandparent | No | Yes | No |
| Other family / friend | No | Yes | No |
| The child (under 16) | No | Yes (with parental help) | No |
| The child (16–17) | Yes (can open their own) | Yes | Yes |

- Only the **registered contact** (parent/guardian who opened the account) can manage the account (change details, transfer providers).
- At age 16, the child can become the registered contact but still cannot withdraw until 18.

## Access and Maturity

- **No withdrawals** are permitted before the child's 18th birthday, except in the event of:
  - Terminal illness of the child
  - Death of the child
- On the child's **18th birthday**, the Junior ISA automatically converts into an adult **ToggleBank Cash ISA**:
  - The child gains full control of the account
  - All funds remain tax-free within the ISA wrapper
  - The account continues to earn interest at the prevailing Cash ISA rate
  - The child can withdraw, transfer, or continue saving

## Interest Details

- The current rate is **4.00% AER (variable)**, applied to the full balance.
- Interest is calculated daily and credited annually on the anniversary of the account opening.
- The rate is variable and may change. We will give at least **60 days' notice** of any reduction.

## Transferring a Junior ISA

- **Transfer in** from another provider — we handle the process, typically completed within 15 working days. The transfer does not count towards the current year's allowance.
- **Transfer out** to another provider — available at any time by contacting us.
- Only the registered contact can initiate a transfer.

## How to Open

1. **Online** — Open at togglebank.co.uk/junior-isa (parent/guardian must verify identity)
2. **Mobile app** — Apply through the ToggleBank app
3. **In branch** — Visit any ToggleBank branch with the child's birth certificate and the parent/guardian's photo ID
4. **By post** — Download and complete the application form from our website

You will need:
- The child's full name, date of birth, and National Insurance number (if available)
- The parent/guardian's photo ID and proof of address
