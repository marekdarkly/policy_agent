# ToggleBank Banking Terms Glossary

**Document Reference:** TB-REF-GLOSSARY-2026  
**Effective Date:** 1 January 2026  
**Classification:** Customer-Facing

---

## Overview

Banking jargon can be confusing. This glossary explains common banking terms in plain English to help you understand your accounts, statements, and correspondence from ToggleBank.

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **AER (Annual Equivalent Rate)** | The interest rate on a savings account expressed as if interest were paid and compounded once a year. Allows fair comparison between accounts that pay interest at different intervals. Example: an account paying 1% every 6 months has an AER of approximately 2.01%. |
| **APR (Annual Percentage Rate)** | The total cost of borrowing expressed as a yearly percentage, including interest and mandatory fees. Used on loans, credit cards, and overdrafts. A representative APR means at least 51% of applicants receive that rate or better. |
| **APRC (Annual Percentage Rate of Charge)** | Similar to APR but used specifically for mortgages. Includes all mandatory costs over the full term of the mortgage, including the reversion to SVR. |
| **Arranged Overdraft** | An overdraft facility agreed in advance with your bank, with a set limit. Interest is charged at the agreed rate. |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Balance Transfer** | Moving an existing debt (usually from a credit card) to a new credit card, often to take advantage of a lower or 0% interest rate for a promotional period. A balance transfer fee (typically 1%–3%) may apply. |
| **Bank of England Base Rate** | The interest rate set by the Bank of England's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC). It influences the rates banks charge on loans and pay on savings. Currently 4.00% (as of March 2026). |
| **BACS (Bankers' Automated Clearing Services)** | The system used to process Direct Debits and credits (e.g. salary payments) in the UK. BACS payments take 3 working days to clear. |
| **BIC (Bank Identifier Code)** | An 8 or 11-character code that identifies a specific bank for international transfers. Also known as a SWIFT code. |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **CHAPS (Clearing House Automated Payment System)** | A same-day payment system for high-value sterling transfers within the UK. Typically used for house purchases and large commercial payments. ToggleBank charges £25 per CHAPS payment. |
| **Compound Interest** | Interest calculated on both the original deposit and any interest already earned. Over time, compound interest grows your savings faster than simple interest. |
| **Confirmation of Payee** | A name-checking service that verifies the recipient's name when you set up a new payment, helping prevent fraud and misdirected payments. |
| **Credit Score** | A numerical rating (typically 0–999 depending on the agency) that represents your creditworthiness. Based on your credit history, payment behaviour, and other factors. Each credit reference agency uses its own scoring model. |

## D–E

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Direct Debit** | An instruction you give to your bank allowing a company to collect varying amounts from your account on agreed dates. Covered by the Direct Debit Guarantee. Common for utility bills, subscriptions, and council tax. |
| **EAR (Equivalent Annual Rate)** | The interest rate on a borrowing product (e.g. overdraft) expressed as an annual rate. Similar to APR but typically excludes fees. |
| **Equity** | In property terms, the difference between your home's current market value and the outstanding mortgage balance. Example: a home worth £300,000 with a £200,000 mortgage has £100,000 equity. |

## F–G

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Faster Payments** | A UK payment system that transfers money between bank accounts in near real-time (typically seconds), available 24/7. Most UK banks support Faster Payments for transfers up to £250,000. |
| **Fixed Rate** | An interest rate that stays the same for a set period, regardless of changes to the Bank of England Base Rate. Provides certainty on repayments (mortgages) or returns (savings). |
| **FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme)** | The UK's deposit insurance scheme. Protects deposits up to £85,000 per person per authorised institution if the bank fails. |
| **Gross Rate** | The interest rate before tax is deducted. Since April 2016, interest is paid gross (without tax deducted at source) — you may owe tax depending on your Personal Savings Allowance. |

## I–L

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **IBAN (International Bank Account Number)** | A standardised international numbering system used to identify bank accounts across borders. UK IBANs are 22 characters long and start with "GB". |
| **ISA (Individual Savings Account)** | A tax-efficient savings or investment wrapper. Interest earned or investment growth within an ISA is free from Income Tax and Capital Gains Tax. Annual allowance: £20,000 (2025/26). |
| **LTV (Loan-to-Value)** | The size of your mortgage expressed as a percentage of the property's value. Example: a £180,000 mortgage on a £200,000 property = 90% LTV. Lower LTV typically means better interest rates. |

## M–N

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Mortgage** | A loan secured against a property. If repayments are not maintained, the lender may repossess the property. Typical terms are 25–35 years. |
| **MPC (Monetary Policy Committee)** | The committee of the Bank of England responsible for setting the UK Base Rate. Meets eight times per year. |
| **Net Rate** | The interest rate after tax has been deducted. Since April 2016, UK savings interest is paid gross; "net" is now less commonly used but may appear in older documentation. |

## O–P

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Overdraft** | A facility that allows you to spend more than the balance in your current account, up to an agreed limit. Interest is charged on the overdrawn amount. |
| **PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2)** | EU regulation (adopted in UK law) that introduced Open Banking, Strong Customer Authentication, and gave customers the right to share their financial data with regulated third-party providers. |
| **Personal Savings Allowance** | The amount of savings interest you can earn tax-free each year. Basic rate taxpayers: £1,000. Higher rate: £500. Additional rate: £0. |

## R–S

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Repayment Mortgage** | A mortgage where each monthly payment covers both interest and a portion of the capital borrowed. The full loan is repaid by the end of the term. |
| **SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area)** | A payment integration scheme that simplifies euro bank transfers between participating countries (EU/EEA + some others). SEPA transfers are typically same-day and lower-cost than SWIFT. |
| **Standing Order** | A fixed, regular payment you instruct your bank to make on a set date. Unlike a Direct Debit, the amount is always the same and is controlled by you. |
| **SVR (Standard Variable Rate)** | The default variable interest rate your mortgage reverts to after a fixed or discounted rate period ends. Typically higher than promotional rates. |
| **SWIFT** | The international messaging network used to send payment instructions between banks worldwide. "SWIFT code" and "BIC" are used interchangeably. |
| **Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)** | A security requirement under PSD2 that requires two or more independent verification factors (e.g. password + fingerprint) for electronic payments and account access. |

## T–V

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Term Deposit / Fixed-Term Bond** | A savings account where your money is locked away for a set period (e.g. 1, 2, or 3 years) in exchange for a guaranteed interest rate. Early withdrawal usually incurs a penalty. |
| **Tracker Mortgage** | A variable-rate mortgage that tracks the Bank of England Base Rate at a set margin above it. Example: Base Rate + 1.50% = 5.50% when the Base Rate is 4.00%. |
| **Variable Rate** | An interest rate that can change over time, typically linked to the Bank of England Base Rate or the lender's SVR. |
| **VRP (Variable Recurring Payments)** | An Open Banking feature that allows recurring payments of varying amounts, controlled by the payer with pre-set limits. More flexible than Direct Debits. |
