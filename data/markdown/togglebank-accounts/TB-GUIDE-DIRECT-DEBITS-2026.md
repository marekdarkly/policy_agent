## Overview

Direct Debits and standing orders are convenient ways to make regular payments. This guide explains how they work, how to manage them, and what protection you have.

## Direct Debits vs Standing Orders

| Feature | Direct Debit | Standing Order |
|---|---|---|
| Who controls the amount? | The **recipient** (company you're paying) | **You** (fixed amount each time) |
| Amount can vary? | Yes — the company can adjust the amount | No — same amount every time |
| Common uses | Utility bills, subscriptions, council tax, insurance | Rent, regular savings transfers, club memberships |
| Protection | Direct Debit Guarantee | No equivalent guarantee |
| Set up by | You give permission; the company arranges collection | You set it up directly with your bank |
| Cancellation | Cancel with your bank or the company | Cancel with your bank |

## Setting Up a Direct Debit

1. Provide your **sort code and account number** to the company requesting payment.
2. Complete a **Direct Debit Instruction** (paper or electronic mandate).
3. The company submits the instruction to ToggleBank — you will see it listed in your Online Banking or Mobile App within **3 working days**.
4. You will receive an **Advance Notice** from the company at least **10 working days** before the first collection (or when the amount or date changes), unless you have agreed otherwise.

## The Direct Debit Guarantee

All Direct Debits are covered by the **Direct Debit Guarantee**, which protects you if:

- A payment is collected in **error** (wrong amount or wrong date).
- A payment is collected **without proper advance notice**.

Under the Guarantee:

- You are entitled to a **full and immediate refund** from ToggleBank.
- You can claim the refund by contacting us — no need to contact the company first.
- There is **no time limit** on claiming under the Guarantee.

## Setting Up a Standing Order

You can set up a standing order via:

- **Online Banking** — Payments > Standing Orders > Create New
- **Mobile App** — Payments > Standing Orders > Add
- **In-branch** or **by phone**

You will need to specify:

- Recipient account details (sort code, account number, name)
- **Amount** (fixed)
- **Frequency** (weekly, fortnightly, monthly, quarterly, annually)
- **Start date** and optional **end date**

## Amending & Cancelling

| Action | Direct Debit | Standing Order |
|---|---|---|
| Amend amount | Contact the collecting company | Edit in Online Banking or Mobile App |
| Change date | Contact the collecting company | Edit in Online Banking or Mobile App |
| Cancel | Cancel via Online Banking, Mobile App, phone, or in-branch | Cancel via Online Banking, Mobile App, phone, or in-branch |
| Notice period | Takes effect **immediately** (future collections stopped) | Takes effect **immediately** |

**Important:** Cancelling a Direct Debit with ToggleBank does **not** cancel any underlying contract you have with the company. You should inform the company separately.

## Viewing Upcoming Payments

In the Mobile App and Online Banking, you can view:

- A **calendar view** of all upcoming Direct Debit and standing order payments for the next 30 days.
- The **estimated total** of upcoming payments, to help you ensure sufficient funds.
- **Historical payments** — full history of all past Direct Debit and standing order transactions.

## Failed Payments

If a Direct Debit or standing order fails due to **insufficient funds**:

- You will receive an **instant notification** via push alert and email.
- ToggleBank's **retry policy**: failed Direct Debits may be retried by the collecting company, typically within **3 working days**. ToggleBank does not charge a fee for failed Direct Debits.
- A failed standing order will **not** be automatically retried — you must ensure funds are available for the next scheduled date.
- Repeated failed payments may be reported to **credit reference agencies** and could affect your credit score.

## Indemnity Claims

If you believe a Direct Debit payment was taken incorrectly, you can submit an **indemnity claim**:

1. Contact ToggleBank via phone, secure message, or in-branch.
2. Provide the date, amount, and company name.
3. ToggleBank will process the refund — typically returned to your account **within 2 working days**.
4. The collecting company may dispute the claim, in which case ToggleBank will contact you with further details.
