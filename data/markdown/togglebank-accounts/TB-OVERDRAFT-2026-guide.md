**Product Code:** TB-OVERDRAFT-2026

## What is an Overdraft?

An overdraft lets you borrow money through your current account when your balance goes below zero. It's designed to cover short-term shortfalls, not as a long-term borrowing solution. ToggleBank offers arranged overdrafts on our Everyday Current Account and Premium Current Account, each with different rates and features.

| Feature | Everyday Current Account | Premium Current Account |
|---|---|---|
| Interest rate (EAR) | 39.9% EAR (variable) | 15.9% EAR (variable) |
| Equivalent daily rate | Approx. 0.093% per day | Approx. 0.040% per day |
| Interest-free buffer | First £25 | First £250 |
| Maximum arranged limit | £3,000 | £10,000 |
| Monthly fee | None | Account fee of £15/month (covers all Premium benefits) |
| Application | App or Online Banking | App, Online Banking, or via your relationship manager |

Interest is calculated daily on the overdrawn balance (after the buffer) and charged monthly, on the first working day of each month.

## How to Apply for an Arranged Overdraft

1. **Check eligibility** — Open the ToggleBank app or log in to Online Banking. Go to your current account and select "Overdraft." We'll show you whether you're eligible and the maximum limit available.
2. **Set your limit** — Choose a limit up to the maximum offered. You can select any amount in £50 increments.
3. **Confirm and activate** — Your overdraft is available immediately after approval. No paperwork or branch visit required.

You can also apply by calling 0345 600 7000 or visiting any ToggleBank branch.

Approval is subject to a credit check and affordability assessment. Not all applications will be approved.

## Overdraft Alerts

ToggleBank sends automatic alerts to help you stay in control of your overdraft:

| Alert | Trigger | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Approaching limit | Balance reaches 80% of your arranged overdraft limit | Push notification, SMS, and email |
| At limit | Balance reaches 100% of your arranged overdraft limit | Push notification, SMS, and email |
| Exceeded limit | Balance goes beyond your arranged limit (unarranged borrowing) | Push notification, SMS, and email |
| Monthly interest charge | Interest has been applied to your account | In-app notification |

You can manage your alert preferences in the ToggleBank app under Settings > Notifications.

## Managing Your Overdraft

### Tips for Reducing Your Overdraft

- **Set a budget** — Use the ToggleBank app's spending insights to track where your money goes and identify areas to cut back.
- **Set up a regular transfer** — Arrange a standing order to pay a fixed amount back into your account each payday.
- **Reduce your limit gradually** — As you pay down your overdraft, reduce your limit to avoid the temptation to borrow again.
- **Consider a personal loan** — If you're regularly overdrawn by £1,000 or more, a personal loan at 6.9%–9.9% APR may be significantly cheaper than an overdraft at 39.9% EAR.
- **Speak to us** — If you're struggling, our Money Support team can help. Call 0345 600 7000 or visit togglebank.co.uk/money-worries.

### Increasing or Decreasing Your Limit

- **Increase:** Request an increase through the ToggleBank app, Online Banking, or by phone. Subject to a credit check and affordability assessment.
- **Decrease:** You can reduce your limit at any time through the app or Online Banking. This takes effect immediately. You cannot reduce your limit below your current overdrawn balance.
- **Removing your overdraft:** You can cancel your arranged overdraft at any time, provided your balance is £0 or in credit.

## What Happens If You Exceed Your Limit?

If your balance goes beyond your arranged overdraft limit, this is called **unarranged borrowing**. Since April 2020 (in line with FCA rules), ToggleBank does not charge a higher rate for unarranged borrowing — the same interest rate applies. However:

- We may decline transactions that would take you beyond your arranged limit
- Sustained unarranged borrowing may be reported to credit reference agencies
- We'll contact you to discuss how to return your account to within its arranged limit

## Persistent Overdraft (FCA Rules)

Under FCA regulations, if you have been continuously overdrawn for **12 months**, ToggleBank is required to contact you to discuss options for repaying your overdraft more affordably. If you remain continuously overdrawn for **18 months**, we must take further steps, which may include:

- Offering a structured repayment plan
- Reducing or removing your overdraft facility
- Suspending or reducing interest and charges

These rules are designed to protect customers from the cost of long-term overdraft use. If you've been in your overdraft for an extended period, we encourage you to contact us to explore more cost-effective borrowing options.

## Overdraft vs Personal Loan

If you need to borrow for more than a few weeks, a personal loan is usually much cheaper than an overdraft. Here's a comparison:

| Borrowing £2,000 for 12 Months | Everyday Overdraft (39.9% EAR) | Personal Loan (9.9% APR) |
|---|---|---|
| Approximate total interest | £667 | £108 |
| Monthly cost | £55.63 (interest only, balance unchanged) | £175.90 (balance reduces to £0) |
| Balance after 12 months | £2,000 (still owed) | £0 (fully repaid) |

A personal loan gives you a clear repayment plan with a fixed end date, while an overdraft has no structured repayment schedule.

## Important Information

- ToggleBank is authorised by the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA) and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and PRA. Financial Services Register number: 100234.
- Eligible deposits with ToggleBank are protected by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS) up to £85,000 per depositor.
- An overdraft is repayable on demand. ToggleBank may ask you to repay all or part of your overdraft at any time.
- Using an overdraft may affect your credit score. Regular or prolonged overdraft use is visible to other lenders when they check your credit file.
- Interest rates are variable and may change. EAR stands for Equivalent Annual Rate and shows the annual rate of interest, taking into account the effect of compounding.
