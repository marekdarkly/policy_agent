Adding the fee to the loan means you pay interest on it over the mortgage term. On a £999 fee added to a 25-year mortgage at 4.29%, this would cost an additional £722 in interest over the full term.

## Representative Examples

- **First-time buyer:** A mortgage of £200,000 payable over 25 years on a capital repayment basis at a fixed rate of 4.29% for 2 years, then reverting to the SVR of 6.99%, would require 24 monthly payments of £1,089.89 followed by 276 monthly payments of £1,415.18. Total amount payable: £417,033.84. **APRC 6.5%.**
- **Remortgage:** A mortgage of £250,000 payable over 20 years at a fixed rate of 3.89% for 5 years, then reverting to the SVR of 6.99%, would require 60 monthly payments of £1,497.22 followed by 180 monthly payments of £1,822.74. Total amount payable: £417,726.40. **APRC 5.9%.**
- **Buy-to-let:** A mortgage of £150,000 payable over 25 years on an interest-only basis at a fixed rate of 5.29% for 2 years, then reverting to the BTL SVR of 7.49%, would require 24 monthly payments of £661.25 followed by 276 monthly payments of £936.25, plus a final capital repayment of £150,000. Total amount payable: £424,425.00. **APRC 7.3%.**
