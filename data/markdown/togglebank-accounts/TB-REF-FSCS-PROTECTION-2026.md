## Overview

The **Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)** is the UK's statutory deposit insurance scheme. It protects your money if a bank, building society, or credit union authorised by the Prudential Regulation Authority fails. ToggleBank is fully covered by the FSCS.

## Deposit Protection

| Coverage | Limit |
|---|---|
| Per eligible depositor, per institution | **£85,000** |
| Joint accounts | **£170,000** (£85,000 per person) |
| Temporary high balance | **Up to £1,000,000** for 6 months |

### How the £85,000 Limit Works

- The limit applies **per person, per authorised institution** — not per account.
- If you hold multiple accounts with ToggleBank (e.g. current account, savings account, ISA), the balances are **combined** for FSCS purposes.
- ToggleBank operates as a **single FSCS-protected entity**. We do not share our FSCS limit with any other banking brand.
- Deposits held in different currencies are converted to GBP at the prevailing exchange rate and counted towards the £85,000 limit.

### Joint Accounts

Each joint account holder is treated as a **separate depositor**. A joint account with a balance of up to £170,000 is fully protected (£85,000 per person). Each person's share of the joint account is combined with any sole accounts they hold.

## Temporary High Balance Protection

If your account balance temporarily exceeds £85,000 due to certain qualifying life events, you may be covered for up to **£1,000,000** for **6 months** from the date the funds were deposited.

Qualifying events include:

- **Sale of a residential property** (proceeds from a house sale)
- **Inheritance** or bequest
- **Redundancy** or voluntary severance payment
- **Insurance payout** (e.g. life insurance, critical illness, property damage)
- **Divorce or dissolution** of a civil partnership (financial settlement)
- **Retirement lump sum** (pension drawdown or commutation)
- **Personal injury compensation**
- **Benefits paid by a government body**

You may need to provide **supporting evidence** (e.g. solicitor letter, probate documents, insurance correspondence) if you need to make a claim during the temporary high balance period.

## Other FSCS Protection

The FSCS covers more than just deposits:

| Product Type | Coverage Limit |
|---|---|
| **Deposits** (current accounts, savings, ISAs) | £85,000 per person per institution |
| **Investments** (stocks, shares, unit trusts, investment advice) | £85,000 per person per firm |
| **Insurance** (general insurance claims) | 90% of the claim, no upper limit |
| **Insurance** (compulsory insurance, e.g. motor third-party) | 100% of the claim, no upper limit |
| **Mortgage advice** | £85,000 per person per firm |

Note: Investment protection covers situations where a firm has gone out of business and cannot return your assets, or has given you negligent advice. It does **not** protect against investment losses due to market performance.

## How to Claim

If ToggleBank were to fail (which we work hard to ensure never happens):

1. **FSCS contacts you** — In most cases, FSCS will contact eligible depositors directly. You do not need to apply proactively.
2. **Automatic payout** — For deposits, FSCS aims to pay out within **7 calendar days** of the firm failing. Payments are typically made to an alternative bank account or by cheque.
3. **Make a claim** — If you are not contacted automatically, or for non-deposit claims, you can submit a claim at [fscs.org.uk/make-a-claim](https://fscs.org.uk/make-a-claim) or call **0800 678 1100**.

### Claim Timeline

| Claim Type | Expected Payout Timeline |
|---|---|
| Deposits (straightforward) | Within **7 calendar days** |
| Deposits (complex, e.g. trusts, temporary high balances) | Within **3 months** |
| Investments | Within **6 months** (complex cases may take longer) |
| Insurance | Varies depending on the claim type |

## What Is Not Covered

The FSCS does **not** cover:

- Deposits in **non-EEA branches** of UK banks (unless specifically opted in)
- Deposits by **financial institutions** and **large companies** that exceed the Companies Act small company thresholds
- **Cryptocurrencies** or unregulated digital assets
- **Peer-to-peer lending** balances (unless the platform is FCA-authorised with FSCS cover)
