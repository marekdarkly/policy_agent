## Borrowing and Affordability

### How much can I borrow?

As a general guide, we lend up to **4.5 times your gross annual income** (or combined income for joint applications). Applicants earning over £75,000 per annum may be eligible for up to **5.5 times income**, subject to a detailed affordability assessment. The maximum loan is £1,000,000.

Your actual borrowing amount depends on:

- Your income and employment status
- Existing financial commitments (loans, credit cards, child maintenance)
- Monthly living costs and essential expenditure
- Credit history and credit score
- Deposit size and the property's value

Use our online calculator at togglebank.co.uk/mortgage-calculator for an indicative figure, or speak to an adviser for a personalised assessment.

## Repayment and Overpayments

### Can I overpay my mortgage?

Yes. You can overpay up to **10% of the outstanding balance per year** without incurring early repayment charges on all fixed rate products. Overpayments reduce the total interest you pay and can shorten your mortgage term. There is no overpayment limit once you are on the SVR or on a tracker product without ERCs.

### What happens when my fixed rate deal ends?

Your mortgage will move to the ToggleBank Standard Variable Rate (SVR), currently **6.99%**. This is likely to increase your monthly payments. We'll contact you approximately 3 months before your deal ends to offer you a new fixed or tracker rate. You can also switch at any time through Online Banking, the app, or by speaking to an adviser. There is no charge to switch to a new ToggleBank deal.

### What is the difference between repayment and interest-only?

- **Repayment (capital and interest):** Each monthly payment covers the interest charge plus a portion of the loan itself. By the end of the term, the mortgage is fully repaid. This is the most common and lowest-risk option.
- **Interest-only:** Monthly payments cover only the interest. The full loan balance remains outstanding and must be repaid at the end of the term. You need a credible repayment plan (e.g., investments, savings, sale of another property). Interest-only is not available above 75% LTV for residential mortgages.

## Rates and Charges

### What is the difference between a fixed rate and a tracker?

- **Fixed rate:** Your interest rate is locked for a set period (typically 2 or 5 years). Your monthly payments stay the same regardless of Bank of England Base Rate changes. Offers certainty and budgeting security.
- **Tracker rate:** Your rate tracks the Bank of England Base Rate by a set margin (e.g., Base Rate + 0.75%). If the Base Rate rises, your payments go up; if it falls, your payments go down. Tracker rates have no early repayment charges but carry more uncertainty.

### What is an ERC (Early Repayment Charge)?

An early repayment charge applies if you repay all or part of your mortgage beyond the 10% annual overpayment allowance during a fixed rate period. ERCs are typically calculated as a percentage of the amount repaid early:

| Time Remaining on Deal | Typical ERC |
|---|---|
| More than 4 years | 5% |
| 3–4 years | 4% |
| 2–3 years | 3% |
| 1–2 years | 2% |
| Less than 1 year | 1% |

No ERCs apply after your deal ends and you move to the SVR.

### What is an offset mortgage?

An offset mortgage links your savings to your mortgage. Your savings balance is offset against your mortgage balance, so you only pay interest on the difference. For example, if you have a £200,000 mortgage and £30,000 in savings, you pay interest on £170,000. You don't earn interest on your savings, but the tax-free interest saving can be more valuable, particularly for higher-rate taxpayers. ToggleBank offset mortgages are available — contact us for current rates.

### Can I port my mortgage?

Yes. If you're moving home, you can transfer (port) your existing mortgage deal to a new property, subject to a new valuation and affordability assessment. This means you keep your current rate and avoid ERCs. If you need to borrow more, the additional amount will be on a new product at current rates.

## Buying Costs and Schemes

### What are the current stamp duty thresholds?

Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) applies to property purchases in England and Northern Ireland:

| Purchase Price Band | SDLT Rate (Main Residence) | SDLT Rate (Additional Property) |
|---|---|---|
| Up to £250,000 | 0% | 3% |
| £250,001 – £925,000 | 5% | 8% |
| £925,001 – £1,500,000 | 10% | 13% |
| Over £1,500,000 | 12% | 15% |

First-time buyers pay 0% on the first £425,000 and 5% on the portion from £425,001 to £625,000 (property must not exceed £625,000 to qualify for relief).

### What is Help to Buy?

The Help to Buy equity loan scheme (England) closed to new applications in October 2022 and all completions finalised by March 2023. If you have an existing Help to Buy equity loan, ToggleBank can help you remortgage — including raising capital to repay the equity loan. Contact our mortgage advisers for guidance.

### What is Shared Ownership?

Shared Ownership allows you to buy a share of a property (between 25% and 75%) and pay rent on the remaining share. ToggleBank offers mortgages on Shared Ownership properties. You can staircase (buy additional shares) over time. Eligibility is managed by the housing association, and household income must generally be below £80,000 (£90,000 in London).
