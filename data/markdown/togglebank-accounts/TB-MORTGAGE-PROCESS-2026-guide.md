# ToggleBank Mortgage Application Guide

**Product Code:** TB-MORTGAGE-PROCESS-2026
**Last Updated:** January 2026
**Applies to:** All ToggleBank residential mortgage products

## Overview

Buying a home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make. This guide walks you through each stage of the mortgage process, from your initial enquiry to picking up the keys on completion day.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Decision in Principle (DIP)

A Decision in Principle (also called an Agreement in Principle or Mortgage in Principle) confirms how much ToggleBank is willing to lend you, based on a preliminary assessment of your income and credit history.

- **How to apply:** Online at togglebank.co.uk/mortgages, by phone (0345 600 7000), or in any branch
- **What you need:** Basic income details, employment information, and consent for a soft credit check
- **Timeframe:** Typically **15 minutes** for an online or phone application
- **Validity:** 90 days from issue
- **Impact on credit score:** A DIP uses a soft credit search, which is not visible to other lenders

A DIP is not a guarantee of lending — a full affordability assessment and property valuation will follow.

### Step 2: Property Search

With your DIP in hand, you can house-hunt with confidence. Estate agents and sellers often prefer buyers who can demonstrate they have mortgage funding agreed in principle.

- Use your DIP to show sellers and agents you are a serious buyer
- Remember to budget for additional costs: stamp duty, solicitor fees, survey, removal costs, and furnishing
- Once you find a property, make an offer — your estate agent will handle negotiations

### Step 3: Full Mortgage Application

Once your offer on a property has been accepted, it's time to submit your full mortgage application.

**Documents checklist:**

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| Proof of identity | Valid passport or UK driving licence |
| Proof of address | Utility bill or council tax bill (within 3 months) |
| Income evidence (employed) | 3 months' payslips and latest P60 |
| Income evidence (self-employed) | 2–3 years' SA302 tax calculations and tax year overviews |
| Bank statements | 3 months' statements for all current accounts |
| Proof of deposit | Savings account statements, gift letter if deposit includes a gift |
| Solicitor details | Name and contact details of your chosen conveyancer |
| Property details | Full address and estate agent details |

- **How to apply:** Your ToggleBank mortgage adviser will help you complete the application online, by phone, or in-branch
- **Timeframe:** Allow **1–2 hours** to complete and submit

### Step 4: Valuation

ToggleBank instructs a RICS-qualified surveyor to carry out a valuation of the property. This is to confirm the property is adequate security for the mortgage.

- **Standard valuation:** Free on all ToggleBank mortgage products — paid for by ToggleBank
- **Timeframe:** Typically arranged within **3–5 working days** of application submission; report returned within 2–3 working days of the visit
- **Important:** The standard valuation is not a structural survey. We strongly recommend commissioning your own homebuyer's report or full building survey for your own peace of mind

| Survey Type | Purpose | Typical Cost |
|---|---|---|
| ToggleBank valuation (free) | Confirms property value for lending purposes | £0 (included) |
| RICS HomeBuyer Report | Identifies significant issues, includes valuation | £400–£700 |
| Full Building Survey | Comprehensive structural inspection | £600–£1,500 |

### Step 5: Mortgage Offer

Once the valuation is satisfactory and underwriting is complete, ToggleBank issues a formal mortgage offer.

- **Timeframe:** Typically **5–10 working days** after valuation
- **Validity:** The offer is normally valid for **6 months**
- **Contents:** The offer letter sets out the loan amount, interest rate, term, monthly payments, and any special conditions
- A copy is sent to your solicitor/conveyancer to proceed with legal work

### Step 6: Conveyancing and Exchange of Contracts

Your solicitor (conveyancer) handles the legal transfer of the property. This includes:

- Conducting local authority searches (planning, environmental, drainage)
- Reviewing the title deeds and property information forms
- Raising enquiries with the seller's solicitor
- Reporting to you on the terms of the purchase
- Arranging buildings insurance (required from exchange)

**Exchange of contracts** is the point at which the purchase becomes legally binding. You pay your deposit (usually 10% of the purchase price) and agree a completion date.

- **Timeframe:** Conveyancing typically takes **4–8 weeks** but can vary significantly depending on the chain and search turnaround times

### Step 7: Completion

Completion is the day you become the legal owner of the property and collect the keys.

**What happens on completion day:**

1. Your solicitor requests the mortgage funds from ToggleBank (usually sent by CHAPS same-day transfer)
2. Your solicitor transfers the full purchase price to the seller's solicitor
3. Once funds are confirmed, the seller's solicitor authorises release of the keys
4. You collect the keys from the estate agent — the property is now yours
5. Your solicitor registers the property and mortgage with HM Land Registry
6. Stamp Duty Land Tax must be filed and paid within 14 days of completion

## What Can Delay a Mortgage?

- Incomplete or missing documents (most common cause of delay)
- Property chain issues — if the seller is also buying, delays can cascade
- Slow local authority searches (some councils take 4–6 weeks)
- Valuation issues — if the property is valued below the purchase price, you may need to renegotiate or increase your deposit
- Lease issues on leasehold properties (short lease, missing freeholder consent)
- Changes in circumstances during the application (changing jobs, taking on new credit)

## Tips for a Smooth Process

- Gather all documents before you apply — this is the single biggest factor in avoiding delays
- Respond promptly to any requests from your adviser, solicitor, or the underwriting team
- Avoid making major financial changes during the application (do not take on new debt, change jobs, or make large unexplained deposits)
- Instruct a solicitor as soon as your offer is accepted — don't wait for the mortgage offer
- Consider a homebuyer's report or building survey for older or unusual properties
