## Overview

The ToggleBank Lifetime ISA helps you save for your **first home** or **retirement** with a generous **25% government bonus** on top of your contributions. Our Cash LISA pays a competitive interest rate on your savings, and the government tops up every contribution you make — up to £1,000 free per year.

## How the Government Bonus Works

Every time you contribute to your Lifetime ISA, the government adds a **25% bonus** within 4–9 weeks:

| Monthly Contribution | Annual Contribution | Government Bonus | Total Saved Per Year |
|---|---|---|---|
| £100 | £1,200 | £300 | £1,500 |
| £200 | £2,400 | £600 | £3,000 |
| £333 | £4,000 | £1,000 | £5,000 |

- The bonus is paid directly into your LISA by HMRC.
- You continue to earn interest on both your contributions and the bonus.
- The maximum lifetime government bonus is uncapped — save £4,000 every year from age 18 to 50 and receive up to **£32,000** in bonuses.

## Using Your LISA

### Buying Your First Home

You can use your LISA savings (including the bonus) towards the purchase of your **first residential property** in the UK, provided:

- The property costs **£450,000 or less**
- You are a **first-time buyer** (you have never owned a property anywhere in the world)
- You buy with a **mortgage** (not a cash purchase)
- The LISA has been open for at least **12 months**
- You use a **conveyancer or solicitor** to handle the purchase

### Retirement

You can withdraw your LISA savings (including all bonuses and interest) **tax-free from age 60** for any purpose.

### Withdrawal Penalty

If you withdraw for any reason **other than** buying your first home or after age 60, a **25% government withdrawal charge** applies. This effectively means you lose the bonus and a small portion of your own contributions.

| Scenario | Withdrawal Treatment |
|---|---|
| First home purchase (qualifying) | Penalty-free |
| Age 60+ withdrawal | Penalty-free |
| Terminal illness (less than 12 months) | Penalty-free |
| Any other withdrawal | **25% penalty** on amount withdrawn |

**Example:** If your LISA balance is £5,000 (£4,000 contributions + £1,000 bonus), an unauthorised withdrawal of the full £5,000 would incur a 25% charge of £1,250. You would receive £3,750.

## Eligibility

- Aged **18–39** to open a new Lifetime ISA
- **UK resident** for tax purposes
- You can contribute until age **50** (after which no further contributions or bonuses are allowed, but the account remains open and continues to earn interest)
- You can hold both a Lifetime ISA and other ISAs in the same tax year

## ISA Allowance

The £4,000 LISA contribution limit counts towards your overall **£20,000 annual ISA allowance**. For example, if you contribute £4,000 to a LISA, you can contribute up to £16,000 across your other ISAs.

## Interest Details

- Interest is calculated daily on the cleared balance (including government bonus) and paid monthly.
- The current rate of **4.00% AER (variable)** may change. We will provide at least 60 days' notice before any rate reduction.
